# Scoring Rubrics

Use these as starting points. If the user or institution provides a rubric, follow that instead.

## Undergraduate CS Thesis or Graduation Design

Total 100:

- Topic significance and problem awareness: 20
- System design and engineering implementation: 25
- Algorithm, theory, or technical rigor: 25
- Experiments, testing, and evidence: 20
- Writing, structure, and formatting: 10

Interpretation:

- 90-100: excellent; clear contribution, strong implementation, rigorous evidence, polished writing.
- 80-89: good; solid work with fixable rigor, evidence, or presentation gaps.
- 70-79: passable; complete but with obvious method, experiment, or writing weaknesses.
- Below 70: risky; central claims or implementation/evidence are insufficient.

## Master's Thesis

Total 100:

- Research question and originality: 20
- Literature positioning: 15
- Methodological rigor: 25
- Experimental/evidence quality: 20
- System/data/reproducibility or empirical execution: 10
- Writing and academic norms: 10

## Empirical AI/ML Paper

Total 100:

- Problem importance and novelty: 15
- Method clarity and correctness: 20
- Baselines and experimental design: 20
- Metrics, statistics, and analysis: 15
- Reproducibility and implementation detail: 10
- Related work and positioning: 10
- Writing and presentation: 10

## Systems or Applied AI Paper

Total 100:

- Problem fit and real-world constraints: 15
- Architecture and implementation depth: 20
- Safety, reliability, and boundary control: 15
- Evaluation design and baselines: 20
- Ablations, diagnostics, and failure analysis: 10
- Deployment/reproducibility evidence: 10
- Writing and presentation: 10

## Recommendation Wording

- **Accept / pass / excellent**: central claims are well supported; remaining issues are minor.
- **Weak accept / good / pass after minor revision**: contribution is meaningful and mostly supported; some evidence or clarity gaps remain.
- **Borderline / major revision**: promising work but central claims need stronger evidence, correction, or reframing.
- **Reject / not ready / risky to defend**: correctness, evidence, novelty, or completeness problems undermine the main claim.

Always justify the score with concrete evidence. Avoid false precision when the paper cannot be fully inspected.
